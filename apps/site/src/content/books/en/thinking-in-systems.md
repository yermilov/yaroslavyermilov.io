Very often, when making decisions, people ignore second-order consequences. Systems thinking is exactly about being able to anticipate them ahead of time and shape your actions accordingly. Why do more meticulous code reviews lead both to better code and to bigger changesets? Why does standardizing internal frameworks lead both to faster development and to an outflow of top engineers?
